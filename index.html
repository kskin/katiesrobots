About Me

I am a Robotics PhD student at the University of Michigan working in the Deep Robot Optical Perception (DROP) Lab. My research interests include perception for underwater robots, mobile robotics, 3D reconstruction, and plenoptic imaging. I hold a M.S. in Robotics from University of Michigan and a B.S.E. in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering with a Certificate in Applications of Computing from Princeton University.

Teaching

EECS 442: Computer Vision -- Fall 2016, Graduate Student Instructor

This course is an introduction to 2D and 3D computer vision offered to upper class undergraduates and graduate students. Topics include camera models, multi-view geometry, stereo reconstruction, low-level image processing methods, segmentation, clustering, and high-level vision techniques such as object recognition.
